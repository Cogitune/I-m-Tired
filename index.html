The Problem

Algorithms reward outrage and speed. Our brains like shortcuts. Put them together and you get a world where anything that feels good can look true.

This site doesn’t tell you what to believe. It shows you how to think for yourself.

Thought built on bedrock. A project devoted to helping humans think clearly in a world of noise, division, and manipulation.

How to Think (Not What to Think)

Core Rules

  • Rule 1: Always ask: who benefits if I believe this?
  • Rule 2: Never trust a short clip without context.
  • Rule 3: Look for the strongest version of the other side.
  • Rule 4: Separate how it makes you feel from whether it’s true.

Case File #1

A viral clip claims: “<insert example claim here>”.

  1. Define the claim: What exactly is being said?
  2. Evidence shown: What do we actually see or hear?
  3. What’s missing: What context, numbers, or sources are not there?
  4. Source check: Who posted this and what do they gain?
  5. Cross-check: What do independent sources say?
  6. Provisional verdict: True / partly true / misleading / unknown.

Tools for Your Brain

Before You Share Anything

  • Is there a source I can actually read, not just a screenshot?
  • Does this mostly make me angry, scared, or proud?
  • Have I seen a serious argument from the other side?
  • Can I explain the claim in my own words without exaggerating it?
