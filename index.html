The Problem

Algorithms reward outrage and speed. Our brains like shortcuts. Put them together and anything that hits the right emotion can start to feel like truth.

Solune is about stepping back — seeing the full orbit, not just the frame someone chose for you. Less “me vs them”, more “what’s actually going on here?”

How to Think (Not What to Think)

Core Rules

  • Rule 1: Always ask: who benefits if I believe this?
  • Rule 2: Never trust a short clip without context.
  • Rule 3: Look for the strongest version of the other side.
  • Rule 4: Separate how it makes you feel from whether it’s true.
  • Rule 5: Be willing to say “I don’t know yet” instead of forcing a team.

Case File #1

A viral clip claims: “<insert example claim here>”.

  1. Define the claim: What exactly is being said, in one clear sentence?
  2. Evidence shown: What do we actually see or hear, and what is added as text, music, or editing?
  3. What’s missing: Time, place, numbers, full quote, other angles, other people?
  4. Source check: Who posted this? What do they gain — money, status, outrage, validation?
  5. Cross-check: What do independent sources or longer-form material say about this?
  6. Provisional verdict: True / partly true / misleading / unknown. Be honest if you still don’t know.

Tools for Your Brain

Before You Share Anything

  • Is there a source I can actually read, not just a screenshot or a caption?
  • Does this mostly make me angry, scared, or proud — more than it makes me think?
  • Have I seen a serious argument from the other side, not just a strawman?
  • Can I explain the claim in my own words without exaggerating it?
  • Would I still believe this if it came from someone on the “other” team?

About Solune

I’m a 24-year-old engineering student who’s tired of watching people get dragged around by low-effort content — TikToks, reels, shorts, whatever format the algorithm spits out next.

Solune is my attempt to build something different: a small corner of the internet where you practice thinking in full — sun and moon, daymind and nightmind, logic and emotion — instead of letting someone else think for you.

This is version one. It will change as I do.
